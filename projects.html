Woodworking Projects:

Bench and Table

This was one of my first projects, I built an outdoor banch and table for my family to use at our cabin in the woods. I built this from pressure treated wood you can buy at any hardware store. Furthermore, I built this with just a drill and a circular saw.

Bird Feeder

I built this birtfeeder for my auunt for christmas one year. I used cedar wood to build this so it would last` outside in all weather conditions. This is built so that you can spread an icing type material for the birds to eat.

Bird House

I built this bird house for my aunt again for Christmas. This house is made out of cedar so that it does well in the weather. I put a copper roof on to enhance styling. Also, I made one side flip open so you can easily clean the inside.

Bookshelf

I built this bookshelf to store my growing collection of books. I built this shelf out of strair treads because they are made out of high quality wood that has a rounded edge already. On the edges without a round edge, I used a handplane to match it. T this point in my woodworking career, I have aquired many more tools to make things much easier. The most helpful tool for this project was my track saw, it made cutting the plywood back much easier! Also, excuse the paint job, this is the only picture I have and it is unfortunately just primed.

Mahogany Phone Holders

I built these nifty phone holders out of the mahogany scraps from my charcuterie board project. These holders are designed to prop up your phone at a comfortable viewing angle, perfect for video calls or watching videos hands-free. The sleek design and rich mahogany finish make them both functional and stylish additions to any desk or nightstand.
